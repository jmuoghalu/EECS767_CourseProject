Economy[edit]

Main article: Economy of the United States
  • IMD International 2012 World Competitiveness Scoreboard, ranked 2 out of 60 economies[1]
  • World Economic Forum 2017–2018 Global Competitiveness Report, ranked 2 out of 144 countries[2]
  • Economist Intelligence Unit 2013 Where to be born Index, ranked 16 out of 80 countries[3]
  • World Economic Forum 2014 Global Enabling Trade Report ranked 15[4]

Education[edit]

Main article: Education in the United States
  • Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development 2015 Programme for International Student Assessment, ranked 40 of 72 in mathematics, 25 of 72 in science, 24 of 72 in reading[5]

Globalization[edit]

Main article: Globalization
  • 2006 Globalization Index (A.T. Kearney) ranked 3rd
  • 2010 KOF Index of Globalization ranked 27

Health[edit]

Main article: Healthcare in the United States

As of 2017[update], the United States life expectancy is 79.8 years at birth, ranking 42nd among 224 nations.[6]

As of 2015[update], Maternal Mortality Ratio is 46th out of the countries ranked. (See Maternal mortality in the United States.)

Military[edit]

  • Vision of Humanity 2015 Global Peace Index ranked 94 out of 162 countries[7]

Politics[edit]

Main article: Politics of the United States
  • Transparency International 2014 Corruption Perceptions Index: ranked 17 out of 175 countries[8]
  • Reporters Without Borders 2015 Press Freedom Index: ranked 43 out of 180 countries[9]
  • Economist Intelligence Unit 2016 Democracy Index: ranked 21 out of 167 countries[10]
  • The Heritage Foundation and The Wall Street Journal 2015 Index of Economic Freedom ranked 11 out of 178 economies[11]
  • Fraser Institute Economic Freedom of the World 2013 Annual Report (Economic Freedom Ratings for 2011) ranked 16 out of 152 countries and territories[12]
  • World Justice Project 2015 Rule of Law Index ranked 18 out of 102 countries and jurisdictions[13]
